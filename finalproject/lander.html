This is Lander Wyoming

Lander Stats:

Population: 7,555

Elevation: 5,538'

Lander is the county seat of Fremont County. It is named for the transcontinental explorer Frederick W. Lander. It is located in central Wyoming at the base of the Wind River Mountains & is home to many engaging outdoor adventures. Some of these include the Rise & Fall of the Popo Agie River, where the Popo Agie disappears underground & re-emerges less than a mile downstream from the base of the mountain. Lander also hosts Wild Iris, a renowned mountain climbing venue. Hiking the back trails of Sinks Canyon will take you to the Falls, a beautiful spot high in the mountains where the snow runoff falls down a smooth boulder, perfect for a slide into a clear, cold pool. This is a favorite spot for visitors after a long warm hike.

Lander's 4th of July

Lander is the best place to be on the Fourth of July. People come from all over the country to be a part of our celebration. We often see visitors from New York & Alaska. Fireworks are legal in Wyoming & in Lander city limits are allowed all day on the 4th of July. They are allowed to be fired year round outside city limits. You can watch the town light up after dark from a vantage point in the Mount Hope Cemetery. If you've ever wondered what the War for Independence looked like, Lander is your place to visit.

Lander is also home to the Lander Valley High School Tigers. LVHS is home to a 3A Boys Swim team for the 25th consecutive season. LVHS has 5 varsity sports, including football, swimming & volleyball, available to its students. Their motto is: "LVHS- When only the best will do!"

Come visit Lander! There's something for everyone. We look forward to seeing you here
